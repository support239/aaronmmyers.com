(The story below is real, but the names have been changed for confidentiality.)

——-——-——-——-——-
Emma and Eden were at each other a lot!

From the moment I walked in, they were arguing…

Emma: I’ve asked you to sit down. Now sit down and be quiet!

Eden: I don’t even see what’s the point.

Emma: Can you just be quiet?!

Eden: This is so stupid! It’s not like he’s going to do anything. (Gesturing towards me.)

Emma: (now talking to me.) I’m sorry, she can be so rude sometimes.

Eden: (staring down her mom, but talking to me) I’m sorry she can be stupid sometimes.

Emma: (now back at her daughter.) Now that’s enough! You sit down! You shut your mouth. And you be respectful!

Emma: (now talking to me, and sighing in shame.) I’m sorry, but this is our life. We fight… Constantly!!

Emma: I try, I really do. But sometimes it’s just too much, and I can’t handle it.

(Eden is scowling constantly. It is obvious that she doesn’t want to be there. Crossed arms, slumped down in the chair. Looking completely bored.)

Aaron: I see. My guess is that you two have been through a lot.

Eden: Yeah, more than you’ll ever know.

Emma: I said that’s enough!

Aaron: I get it… This guy shows up (referring to myself). You don’t know who he is. He shouldn’t pretend to know who you are. And why would you want anything to do with him?

Eden rolled her eyes. Her mom replied…

Emma: We need help.

Aaron: I have an idea. Why don’t we play a game?
Eden: This is dumb.

Aaron: I promise it will help.

Eden rolls her eyes again.

Aaron: I promise.

Aaron: Let’s play a game with three rules. I’m going to be very strict. I’m going to call you out. And if you get an answer wrong, I’m going to let you know.

Eden: Do I have to do this?

Emma: Yes.

Eden lets out a large grunt. She already made it clear now that she didn’t wanna be there.

Aaron: Hang in there. We can do this.

Aaron: We’re going to get you to a level of satisfaction much better than where you are now with each other.

Aaron: You guys aren’t happy, hang in there. We can sort this out and get this mended.

Emma: Okaaaay. I have my doubts, but I’m willing to try.

Eden lets out another sigh and another grunt.

Aaron: Your answer has to follow three rules.
1. What can you do?
2. It’s in your control.
3. It will make you a better person.

Eden: Oh my gosh! Do we have to do this?!

Aaron: We got this… We can do this… Hang in there.

Aaron: OK, Eden, on a scale of 1 to 10, where is your satisfaction level for your relationship between you and your mom?

Eden: She rolls her eyes, her mouth is open, she has a look of disgust… Uhh, a 2.

Aaron: OK Emma… What would you rate your level of satisfaction with the relationship, 0 to 10?

Emma: (she looks at the floor, shrugs, a look of despair) Maybe a 3 or a 4.

Aaron: OK. I appreciate the honesty. This won’t be easy, but I promise we can do it.

Aaron: Eden, you first…

She has her arms folded, her head bowed, and looks up with disdain.

Aaron: Eden, what would make it a 3 out of 10… What could you do, it’s in your control, and it will make you a better person?

Eden: I don’t know.

Aaron: Come on, you got this.

Emma sighs. She looks doubtful this will go anywhere.

Aaron: (looking at Eden) What would make it a 3 out of 10?

Eden: Maybe if she got her stupid butt to school on time and picked me up so I don’t have to wait all day for her stupid butt. (I promise she didn’t say butt ;)

Aaron: Errrghrrrrr! Wrong answer. Rule number 2… You can’t control if your mom gets to school on time.

Eden: This is so dumb! Why are we doing this?!

Her mom smirks, not enough to be obvious. She’s curious where this will go. But she’s not convinced.

Aaron: Come on, you got this… What can you do, it’s in your control and it will make you a better person at a level 3/10?

Eden: (it’s obvious she doesn’t want to answer, but she does) Fine… Maybe while I wait… And notice how I said wait… Because I can’t control if she comes (she says with an attitude). I’ll put in my AirPods and listen to music… There… Happy now?… Yes, I can do that.

Aaron: OK… Nice. Emma, your turn. What would make it a solid four for you?

Emma: Maybe give it another try. I can actually try to show up on time. Sometimes I don’t because honestly, I dread going to pick her up sometimes because it just ends up in an argument. So maybe if I just try to pick her up on time, that would be a 4.

Aaron: I like that. OK, Eden, what would make it a solid 4 for you?

Eden: Yeah, I already knew you were going to ask me… And yes, it’s what I can do. And yes, it’s in my control. And yes, it would make me a better person… While I’m waiting… Because I don’t know if my mom will even show… I can text my friends. And I said I could text them, because I know I can’t control if they text back.

Aaron: Nicely done!

Aaron: Emma, back to you… What would make it a 5?

Emma: When I go to pick her up, I can just be more patient… She’s been through a lot! A lot! No kid should ever have to go through what she’s been through. So if I’m more patient that would be… I’d say a 6.

Aaron: Wow! That’s pretty good!

Aaron: OK Eden, back to you. What would make it a 5?

Eden: Maybe if I just keep my mouth shut when my mom picks me up… Yes I know that I can’t control if my mom picks me up, so I said if she picks me up, I can just keep my mouth shut.

Aaron: OK… But will that actually make you a better person if you keep your mouth shut?

Eden: Actually, yes. Because like my mom said, it usually just ends up in a fight. So if I don’t say anything, it might be better.

Aaron: Interesting. OK. I like that.

Aaron: OK Emma, what would take it to a 7?

Emma: You know, I can try to go into work earlier, try to get off earlier, and I can ask my boss… I don’t know if she will say yes, but I can at least ask my boss if I can go in earlier and get off earlier. It’s something I can control asking, but I don’t know if it will work. But I can try.

Aaron: Wow! And what number would that be out of 10?

Emma: That would be a 10 for me. I can try a little harder. You know, she’s a good kid. And I hate getting up early to go into work early, but I can do it for her.

Aaron: Oh my! That’s a big deal! Eden, what would take it to the next level for you?

Eden: If my mom comes to pick me up (she looks over at her mom and smirks, her mom looks back at her with a smile)… I said if. I can ask how her day was and see how I can help her.

Eden: You know, ever since my dad left five years ago, I’ve hated my life. I’ve blamed my mom. I’ve treated her terribly. I’ve hated the world. I’ve hated my life. I’ve held it against my dad.

Eden: But my mom doesn’t deserve any of that. She’s the one that stayed with me. She’s the one that’s been there for me. Even when I have treated her horribly.

Eden: So maybe I can stop being such a b%!?c. Maybe I can start thinking about her and stop thinking about myself for once. That would be a 10 for me…

She side glances at me with a smirk, and sarcastically says… And yes, that’s in my control.

We all laughed.
